# Market Overview: ADHD & "ADHD-Adjacent" Consumer Apps — What's Actually Selling and Being Downloaded

*Research stream: MARKET. Compiled 2026-06-27. All non-obvious claims cited inline with source URLs.*

---

## 0. How to read this report (data-quality caveat)

Hard numbers in this category are genuinely scarce and inconsistent:

- **Most apps are private and disclose nothing.** Download/MAU/revenue figures below come from three classes of source, in descending order of reliability: (1) company self-reports / founder interviews, (2) third-party app-intelligence estimates (Apptopia, Sensor Tower, getLatka, AppBrain — *modeled estimates, not ground truth*), and (3) SEO "market research" reports, which are the least reliable.
- **The "ADHD apps market size" reports disagree wildly** — 2024/2025 estimates range from ~$1.79B to ~$2.4B with CAGRs of 15–17%, projecting anywhere from $4.06B (2029) to $7.7B (2033). These are syndicated SEO reports of low quality and should be treated as directional only. ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/adhd-apps-market-report); [The Business Research Company](https://www.thebusinessresearchcompany.com/report/attention-deficit-hyperactivity-disorder-adhd-apps-global-market-report); [Market Growth Reports](https://www.marketgrowthreports.com/market-reports/adhd-apps-market-115215))
- **Reportedly 2,800+ new ADHD apps launched globally in 2023** — the category is extremely crowded, and most are zombies with negligible installs. ([datainsightsmarket](https://www.datainsightsmarket.com/reports/adhd-apps-528471))

Where a figure is a third-party *estimate* or self-reported, it is flagged as such.

## 1. Top takeaways

1. **The biggest "ADHD" winners are not ADHD-branded medical apps — they are mainstream focus/wellbeing apps that ADHD users adopt.** Forest (60M+ users, 4M paying) and Endel (~$15.8M est. ARR) dwarf every purpose-built ADHD app in scale. ([Forest/Medium](https://medium.com/@janiceleehs/how-forest-app-ranked-1-in-136-countries-with-4m-paying-users-fd502b9cb63d); [getLatka/Endel](https://getlatka.com/companies/endel.io))
2. **The purpose-built ADHD app that crossed into the mainstream did so via design recognition, not medical claims.** Tiimo (a visual planner for neurodivergent users) won Apple's **iPhone App of the Year 2025**. ([Apple/MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2025/12/04/apple-announces-2025-app-store-award-winners/); [Daring Fireball](https://daringfireball.net/2025/12/2025_app_store_award_winners))
3. **The prescription "digital therapeutic" model failed commercially.** Akili's FDA-cleared EndeavorRx never got reimbursed, revenue collapsed, and the company was sold for $34M after a ~$1B SPAC valuation — a cautionary tale for the "medical-grade ADHD app" thesis. ([FierceBiotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/adhd-game-developer-akili-interactive-be-taken-private-after-being-sold-34m); [STAT](https://www.statnews.com/2024/04/30/akili-interactive-digital-therapeutics-firm-announce-layoffs-restructuring/))
4. **Two monetization models clearly work; a third is contested.** (a) Cheap one-time purchase / low-friction freemium at huge scale (Forest). (b) Premium subscription justified by a strong, daily-used utility (Endel, Sunsama). The contested model is the **high-priced "CBT/coaching" ADHD subscription ($40–48/mo, e.g. Inflow)**, which monetizes well per-user but draws heavy churn and "scam"-flavored complaints.
5. **Churn drivers are remarkably consistent: aggressive paywalls/marketing, billing friction (charged after cancel), reading-heavy/complex onboarding that defeats the ADHD user it targets, and "nagging."** Health & wellness apps already retain poorly (D30 ≈ 3–10%); ADHD apps fight that baseline *plus* a population prone to abandonment. ([Business of Apps](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/health-fitness-app-benchmarks/); [youradhdone](https://youradhdone.com/adhd-app-scam/))

## 2. Market context & segmentation

The consumer "ADHD app" space is really four overlapping product categories, which matters because they monetize and retain very differently:

| Category | Examples | Core value | Typical monetization |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Focus / anti-distraction** | Forest, Focus Bear, Brain Focus, Endel (focus sounds) | Block distraction, sustain attention sessions | One-time purchase (Forest) or subscription (Endel, Focus Bear) |
| **Visual planning / time management** | Tiimo, Llama Life, Sunsama, Routine | Externalize executive function: timeboxing, visual schedules | Freemium subscription |
| **ADHD "coaching" / CBT / community** | Inflow, Numo | Behavior-change program, education, peer accountability | High-price subscription ($8–48/mo) |
| **AI task-breakdown / "second brain"** | Goblin Tools, Saner.ai | Decompose overwhelming tasks; capture & organize | Free/donation (Goblin) or low-cost subscription (Saner) |
| **Clinical / prescription (Rx & OTC)** | EndeavorRx, EndeavorOTC | FDA-authorized cognitive treatment | Rx ($99/mo) → failed; OTC pivot |

ADHD users span all four. The data below shows the **mainstream focus + planning apps are where the scale and money actually are**, while ADHD-branded coaching apps monetize aggressively per user but stay small and churny.

## 3. App-by-app breakdown

### Tier 1 — Scaled winners (the apps actually being downloaded at volume)

#### Forest — focus/anti-phone-addiction (the category's biggest consumer success)
- **Scale:** 60M+ users since 2014; **4M paying users**; ranked **#1 in 136 countries**. Third-party estimate of ~300k downloads and ~$100k revenue in a recent month. ([Forest/Medium #1-in-136](https://medium.com/@janiceleehs/how-forest-app-ranked-1-in-136-countries-with-4m-paying-users-fd502b9cb63d); [forestapp.cc](https://www.forestapp.cc/))
- **Pricing / model:** **One-time purchase, not subscription.** iOS **$3.99 one-time**; Android free-with-ads + **$1.99 one-time** Pro to remove ads/unlock features. Core focus sessions, app blocking, and group focus are usable free. ([forestapp.cc](https://www.forestapp.cc/))
- **Why it succeeds:** Tamagotchi-style gamification (grow a tree; it dies if you leave to use your phone — "weaponized guilt"), social/group focus, real-world tree planting (2M+ trees funded), and a deliberate, iterated UX. A cheap one-time price removes the #1 ADHD churn trigger (subscription fatigue). ([Medium "weaponized guilt"](https://medium.com/@jashsak/how-forest-weaponized-guilt-to-hook-40-million-productivity-seekers-2a9ec6903021))
- **Why it can fail users:** It's a blunt blocker; no ADHD-specific scaffolding. Easy to bypass.

#### Endel — AI focus/sleep soundscapes (the revenue leader)
- **Scale / revenue:** **~$15.8M estimated ARR (2025)**; ~$47.5M valuation (getLatka estimates). A third-party breakdown estimated ~$600k/mo revenue and ~200k downloads/mo. ~144 employees by mid-2025. ([getLatka](https://getlatka.com/companies/endel.io); [LinkedIn growth breakdown](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/growth-hacking-lab-ios-apps_endel-app-breakdown-activity-7381704996687413248-9uKJ))
- **Funding:** ~$22.1M across 5 rounds; **Series B $15M (Apr 2022)** led by Waverley Capital w/ True Ventures; backers include Amazon Alexa Fund and Major Lazer's Jillionaire. ([Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/endel); [getLatka](https://getlatka.com/companies/endel.io))
- **Pricing / model:** Subscription (monthly/annual); **~75% of users reportedly choose the annual plan** — a strong signal of perceived recurring value. Cross-platform: iOS, macOS, Android, Apple Watch, Alexa, Apple TV. ([CanvasBusinessModel](https://canvasbusinessmodel.com/blogs/how-it-works/endel-how-it-works))
- **Why it succeeds:** Not ADHD-branded, but heavily used by ADHD/focus seekers. Daily-utility (you literally press play to work/sleep), defensible tech (generative soundscapes), licensing/partnership revenue beyond the app. High annual-plan mix = low churn.

#### Tiimo — visual planner for neurodivergent users (the ADHD-native breakout)
- **Scale:** "Used by over a million people"; some pages claim **3M+ downloads**. Copenhagen-based; co-designed with ADHD/autism input. ([tiimoapp.com](https://www.tiimoapp.com/); [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/tiimo-ai-planner-to-do/id1480220328))
- **Marquee credential:** **Apple iPhone App of the Year 2025** — the strongest mainstream validation any ADHD-native app has received. ([MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2025/12/04/apple-announces-2025-app-store-award-winners/); [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2025/12/04/apple-announces-winners-of-the-2025-app-store-awards/); [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/04/ai-finds-its-way-into-apples-top-apps-of-the-year/))
- **Pricing / model:** **Freemium** (deliberately, after going freemium from paid). Free tier includes planning, to-do lists, and focus timer; **Tiimo Pro** is monthly or annual, with a **7-day trial available on the yearly plan**. Explicitly **ad-free**. ([Why Tiimo went freemium](https://www.tiimoapp.com/resource-hub/why-tiimo-went-freemium); [FAQ](https://www.tiimoapp.com/faq))
- **Why it succeeds:** Visual/timer-first design genuinely fits how ADHD/autistic users think; "designed by and for neurodivergent people" positioning; ad-free freemium builds trust in a category full of dark patterns.

### Tier 2 — VC-backed, ADHD-specific coaching/community apps (monetize hard, stay small, churn)

#### Inflow — CBT-based ADHD self-management (the highest-priced mainstream ADHD app)
- **Funding:** **~$13.3M total** — $2.3M seed (Jan 2022, Hoxton Ventures) + $11M Series A (Jan 2023, led by Octopus Ventures w/ Hoxton & Route66). Founded London 2020 by Levi Epstein, Seb Isaacs, and Dr. George Sachs. ([TechCrunch $11M](https://techcrunch.com/2023/01/11/inflow-a-platform-for-managing-adhd-through-cbt-raises-11m/); [TechCrunch seed](https://techcrunch.com/2022/01/17/inflow-a-science-based-app-for-adhd-raises-2-3m-seed-led-by-hoxton-ventures/))
- **Scale (soft):** ~220k Android downloads per AppBrain estimate; community of 16k+ cited. No official MAU/revenue disclosed. ([AppBrain](https://www.appbrain.com/app/inflow-adhd/inflow.adhd.productivity))
- **Pricing / model:** **$47.99/month or $199.99/year**, 7-day free trial — among the most expensive in the category. iOS + Android; Health & Fitness + Productivity. ([ChoosingTherapy review](https://www.choosingtherapy.com/inflow-adhd-app-review/))
- **Why it succeeds:** Science/CBT positioning, peer-reviewed feasibility study, structured daily program; strong per-user ARPU funds paid acquisition.
- **Why it churns/fails:** The **price point + reading-heavy modules** are a known mismatch for ADHD users; reviews flag "too much reading," bugs blocking module completion, aggressive marketing, and **billing complaints (charged after canceling)** — the exact pattern that fuels "ADHD app scam" sentiment. ([ChoosingTherapy](https://www.choosingtherapy.com/inflow-adhd-app-review/); [youradhdone "scam"](https://youradhdone.com/adhd-app-scam/))

#### Numo — "cringe-free" gamified ADHD planner + community
- **Funding / scale:** YC-backed; ~$500K seed (2024) plus a $50K grant (Ukrainian/Moldovan funds). Claims to have "helped **500K+** people." Founder Julia (an ADHDer). ([Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/mindist); [leadsontrees](https://www.leadsontrees.com/news/numo-adhd-raises-50k-grantuniting-science-memes-and-cringe-free-joy-for-wild-souls-with-adhd))
- **Pricing / model:** **~$7.99/month** (US; varies by region), 7-day trial. iOS + Android. Gamified to-do list with **social accountability** (public/private tasks, "Karma" points, see others' completions) plus daily tips/affirmations. ([App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/numo-cringe-free-adhd-app/id1628994767); [numo.ai](https://numo.ai/))
- **Why it works (where it does):** Community/social accountability + memey, non-clinical tone fits the "ADHD TikTok" audience; far cheaper than Inflow.
- **Risk:** Gamification + social novelty is high-engagement but historically low-retention; depends on sustained community.

### Tier 3 — Indie / niche planning & focus tools (lean, founder-led, mostly profitable-or-tiny)

#### Llama Life — timeboxing single-task planner
- **Revenue / scale:** ~**$51K ARR (~$4,250 MRR)** per Starter Story; **profitable**; pre-seed ~$100K (Black Sheep Capital + Jason Calacanis/LAUNCH). Founder Marie Ng (diagnosed ADHD as an adult). Grew via "building in public" on Twitter + Product Hunt. ([Starter Story](https://www.starterstory.com/llama-life-breakdown))
- **Pricing / model:** **Free tier; Premium ~$4/month or ~$30/year**, 7-day trial. Web, iOS, (Android). Core idea: timeboxing + one-task-at-a-time focus. ([Starter Story](https://www.starterstory.com/llama-life-breakdown); [llamalife.co](https://llamalife.co/))
- **Why it works:** Cheap, simple, single-purpose — the anti-Inflow. Low price + simplicity directly counter the two biggest churn drivers. Trade-off: tiny revenue ceiling.

#### Focus Bear — anti-distraction + routines for AuDHD
- **Founder / backing:** Jeremy Nagel (late-diagnosed AuDHDer, prior exit); came through Monash/Remarkable accelerator. Revenue undisclosed. ([focusbear.io/about](https://www.focusbear.io/about); [Remarkable demo day](https://www.remarkable.org/blog-posts/focus-bear-2023-demo-day-pitch))
- **Pricing / model:** **Pro ~$9.99/month** (older sources cite ~$5/mo), free trial (no card), plus a **"fair-pricing" option if cost is a barrier**. Cross-device blocking (desktop + mobile) so users can't cheat on one device. ([focusbear.io/pricing](https://www.focusbear.io/pricing); [productivity.directory](https://productivity.directory/focus-bear))
- **Why it works:** "By AuDHDers for AuDHDers" trust; cross-platform blocking + morning/evening routine rituals; fair-pricing reduces price-based churn.

#### Sunsama — premium daily planner (not ADHD-branded but heavily used by ADHD pros)
- **Revenue / scale:** Unofficial estimate ~**$1.5M ARR (2024)**, up from ~$1.1M (2023); **profitable since 2022**; named Wirecutter's "best scheduling app" (2025). ([Business Dive review](https://thebusinessdive.com/sunsama-review); [Sunsama pricing manifesto](https://help.sunsama.com/docs/billing/pricing-manifesto/))
- **Pricing / model:** **Single premium tier** — historically **$20/mo (or $16/mo annual)**; raised in 2026 to **$25/mo ($20/mo annual)** after 5 years flat. **14-day trial, no credit card.** ([Morgen pricing breakdown](https://www.morgen.so/blog-posts/sunsama-pricing); [Sunsama pricing](https://www.sunsama.com/pricing))
- **Why it works:** Deliberate daily-planning ritual; one simple tier; no-card trial. Premium price filters for committed power users (low churn) but caps the addressable market.

#### Routine — tasks + calendar + notes, AI-native
- **Funding / model:** YC W21; **~$2.82M raised** (YC, BoxGroup, Target Global). Paris, founded 2020 (Julien Quintard, Quentin Hocquet). **~€10/mo Pro with a free tier** (rare in this segment). ([YC](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/routine); [Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/routine/__BA49KAsbb-1Yt5ESM7LxqiQhyV1A2qFUapgahQBC2Zk); [routine.co/pricing](https://routine.co/pricing))
- **Note:** Productivity-general, not ADHD-specific, but recurrently recommended to ADHD users for unifying tasks/calendar/notes.

#### Goblin Tools — AI task breakdown ("Magic ToDo") — the free/donation outlier
- **Model:** **Web app is completely free, no signup, no ads, no paywall**, funded by **low-cost mobile apps (a few dollars)** + Ko-Fi/Patreon donations. Built solo by Bram De Buyser. ([goblin.tools/About](https://goblin.tools/About); [mutra pricing](https://mutra.app/compare/pricing/goblin-tools/))
- **Why it matters:** Beloved for breaking overwhelming tasks into steps and tone-adjusting text — a near-perfect ADHD micro-utility. **Caution:** third-party copycats in app stores charge subscriptions for the free tool, which erodes user trust in the whole category. ([mutra](https://mutra.app/compare/pricing/goblin-tools/))

#### Saner.ai — ADHD-friendly AI personal assistant / "second brain"
- **Model:** Founded 2023 (Galvin "Giap" Vo, ADHDer); pre-seed, Techstars Tokyo. **Free tier** (with limits, e.g. 1-min voice cap) + low-cost paid plans. AI assistant "Skai": auto-organizes notes/emails/tasks, semantic search, integrates GPT-4/Claude/Gemini + Google Drive/Slack/Calendar. ([saner.ai](https://www.saner.ai/); [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/saner-ai-ai-second-brain); [aichief review](https://aichief.com/ai-productivity-tools/saner-ai/))
- *Note: one source lists pricing "from $6"; the exact tier and period could not be reliably confirmed — treat as unverified.*

### Clinical / prescription tier — the cautionary tale

#### EndeavorRx / EndeavorOTC (Akili Interactive) — FDA-cleared, commercially failed
- **Product:** First FDA-authorized video-game treatment for pediatric ADHD (cleared **June 15, 2020**); efficacy shown in a Lancet Digital Health RCT (after 4 weeks, ~1/3 of children no longer had a measurable attention deficit on at least one objective measure). ([Wikipedia/Akili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EndeavorRx); [Akili announcement](https://www.akiliinteractive.com/news-collection/akili-announces-endeavortm-attention-treatment-is-now-available-for-children-with-attention-deficit-hyperactivity-disorder-adhd-al3pw))
- **Pricing:** **$99 / 30-day prescription** (patient-assistance program available). ([GoodRx](https://www.goodrx.com/conditions/adhd/does-endeavorrx-adhd-treatment-gamification-work))
- **Commercial outcome (the lesson):** Launched at a ~$1B SPAC valuation but revenue was tiny and shrinking — **$538k (2021) → $323k (2022)** — because payers wouldn't reimburse a prescription game. Pivoted to **EndeavorOTC** (over-the-counter, adults); 2023 revenue ~$1.7M ($1.2M from OTC). Cut ~46% of staff, then **sold to Virtual Therapeutics for ~$34M ($0.434/share)** in 2024. ([FierceBiotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/adhd-game-developer-akili-interactive-be-taken-private-after-being-sold-34m); [STAT layoffs](https://www.statnews.com/2024/04/30/akili-interactive-digital-therapeutics-firm-announce-layoffs-restructuring/); [Healthcare Brew, ditching Rx model](https://www.healthcare-brew.com/stories/2023/09/15/digital-therapeutics-company-akili-ditches-prescription-business-model))
- **Takeaway:** FDA clearance ≠ commercial viability. The **direct-to-consumer (OTC) model out-performed the reimbursement (Rx) model** — a strong signal that consumer ADHD apps should sell directly to users, not chase insurers.

## 4. Pricing & monetization patterns that work

| Pattern | Who proves it | Why it works | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cheap one-time purchase at massive scale** | Forest ($1.99–$3.99) | Eliminates subscription fatigue (the #1 ADHD churn trigger); virality + gamification drive volume | Low ARPU; needs huge install base |
| **Premium subscription on a daily-use utility** | Endel, Sunsama | If the app is opened *every day* to work/sleep/plan, recurring price feels justified; high annual-plan mix (Endel ~75%) crushes churn | Smaller TAM; must earn the habit fast |
| **Ad-free freemium with a generous free tier** | Tiimo | Builds trust in a low-trust category; free tier seeds habit, Pro converts power users | Slower monetization; free-tier cannibalization |
| **Low-price simple subscription** | Llama Life (~$4/mo), Numo (~$8/mo) | Price low enough to be an impulse keep; reduces "is this worth $40?" churn | Tiny revenue ceiling for indies |
| **Free core + cheap app / donations** | Goblin Tools | Maximizes reach & goodwill; sustainable for a solo dev | Not VC-scalable; copycats exploit the free brand |
| **"Fair-pricing" / pay-what-you-can option** | Focus Bear | Removes cost as a churn excuse for a population with income variability | Operational complexity |

**Cross-cutting winners:** free trials are universal (7-day standard; 14-day for higher-priced Sunsama; **no-card trials, as in Sunsama/Focus Bear, signal confidence and reduce "trap" perception**). Annual plans materially reduce churn (Endel ~75% annual).

**The contested model:** the **high-price ADHD "coaching/CBT" subscription ($40–48/mo, Inflow)** monetizes well per converted user and can fund paid acquisition, but it sits on top of the worst churn profile in the category and generates the most "scam/overcharged" backlash.

## 5. Why users churn / why apps fail (consistent across sources)

1. **Subscription fatigue & sticker shock.** $29–48/mo for an app is a recurring decision the user keeps re-litigating; the apps with the lowest churn either charge once (Forest) or stay cheap (Llama Life). ([Morgen ADHD apps](https://www.morgen.so/blog-posts/adhd-productivity-apps); [youradhdone](https://youradhdone.com/adhd-app-scam/))
2. **Billing dark patterns.** Reviews repeatedly report being **charged after canceling within the trial window** or billed after a confirmed cancellation — the single most trust-destroying complaint. ([youradhdone "scam"](https://youradhdone.com/adhd-app-scam/); [ChoosingTherapy/Inflow](https://www.choosingtherapy.com/inflow-adhd-app-review/))
3. **Complexity / reading-heavy onboarding — the core irony.** Apps that demand lots of reading, setup, or multi-step configuration **lose the exact ADHD user they target.** Cited explicitly for Inflow's modules. ([ChoosingTherapy](https://www.choosingtherapy.com/inflow-adhd-app-review/))
4. **Nagging / marketing bombardment.** Users explicitly advise avoiding apps that "bombard with constant marketing"; over-notification backfires with this audience. ([youradhdone](https://youradhdone.com/adhd-app-scam/))
5. **Structural retention gravity.** Even well-built wellness apps retain poorly: health & fitness **Day-30 retention ≈ 3–10%** (D1 ≈ 23%), vs. ~6% all-app D30 average. ADHD apps must beat this baseline while serving an abandonment-prone population. ([Business of Apps benchmarks](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/health-fitness-app-benchmarks/); [enable3 benchmarks](https://enable3.io/blog/app-retention-benchmarks-2025); [Digital Yield Group](https://digitalyieldgroup.com/blog/health-fitness-apps-the-resolutioner-churn-problem/))
6. **Commercial failure even with clinical proof.** EndeavorRx had RCT evidence and FDA clearance and still failed — because reimbursement never came and the consumer didn't perceive $99/mo of value. ([FierceBiotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/adhd-game-developer-akili-interactive-be-taken-private-after-being-sold-34m))

---

## 6. Implications for a new entrant

- **Scale lives in mainstream focus/planning, not medical branding.** The volume leaders (Forest, Endel, Tiimo) win on design, habit, and trust — not on "ADHD treatment" claims.
- **Pick a monetization model that defuses churn by design:** either be *cheap enough to keep without thinking* (Llama Life/Forest) or be a *daily ritual worth a premium* (Endel/Sunsama). Avoid being expensive AND effortful (the Inflow trap).
- **Onboarding must respect the disability:** minimal reading, instant value, no nagging. An ADHD app that requires sustained reading/setup is self-defeating.
- **Trust is a feature:** ad-free, no-card trials, honest cancellation, and "fair-pricing" options are differentiators precisely because the category is full of dark patterns.
- **Sell direct to consumers, not insurers** — the OTC vs. Rx outcome at Akili is the clearest market signal available.
